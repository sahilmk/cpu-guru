Core i Series[2008]

ntel's Core-i3, i5 and i7 processors launched with the Nehalem micro-architecture and the company's 45 nm production process in 2008. The architecture was scaled to 32 nm (Westmere) in 2010 and provided the foundation for Intel processors covering the Celeron, Pentium Core and Xeon brands. Westmere scaled to up to eight cores, up to 3.33 GHz clock speed and up to 2.3 billion transistors.

Westmere was effectively replaced by the 32 nm Sandy Bridge architecture in 2011, which shrunk in 2012 to 22 nm in the Ivy Bridge generation (1.4 billion transistors for quad-core processors).
